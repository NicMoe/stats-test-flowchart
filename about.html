For biostatistics students, textbooks have flowcharts to help determine which statistical test is most appropriate given their data and what question they are trying to answer. However, the flowcharts can get out of hand on paper and don't always offer enough information to help a student reach a conclusion.

I created this tool in Spring 2017 to help make sense of the logic behind the stats test selection process. The design for this tool is based off a tongue-in-cheek programming language selection site made by Bill Broughton. Please feel free to provide feedback on this tool and feel free to contribute to this project.

Once you've determined the appropriate statistical test to use for your analysis, a good resource that include the appropriate code for multiple statistical programming languages is from UCLA's Institute for Digital Research and Education.
